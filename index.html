World Happiness Report

Visualising happiness around the world and looking at factors that affect happiness levels of a country

Happiness Score 2019

Oceania, Europe and North America are among the happiest continents. South America and Asia follow the lead and are moderately happy. Africa lags behind on its happiness levels.

This hints towards some possible relation between the level of development and happiness levels. Some interesting factors that could possibly affect happiness levels might have to do with the wealth, social support, and government trust of a nation. Let's explore these trends further with the interactive visualization below!

Happiness Score distribution

Happiness score is normally distributed with a peak around scores between 5 and 5.5 out of 10. Roughly a sixth of all countries lie within this range. As we go further towards either side we see increasingly lesser number of countries. That means there are very few countries that are extremely happy or unhappy. The majority is mildly content with the way things are.

Do you trust your government?

The big low-lying circles tell us that residents of most countries do not trust their government much. We also see that most countries have a mediocre level of freedom afforded to its citizens to make life choices.

Money does buy happiness!

In general, wealthier nations are likely to be happier than their poorer counterparts due to factors like a sense of security for food, shelter and other basic necessities.
